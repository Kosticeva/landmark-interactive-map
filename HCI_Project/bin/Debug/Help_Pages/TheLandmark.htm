All about the Landmark

The landmark represents one of the natural wonders of the world. Every landmark has its own:

  • ID,
  • Name,
  • Description,
  • Climate,
  • Tourist status,
  • Annual revenue,
  • Information about endangered status, engangered species living in it and location,
  • Date when the wonder was discovered,
  • Landmark type,
  • Picture and
  • Tags
    • .

ID

Every landmark is identified with its ID. The Landmark ID is entered at the topmost text field on the left side of the dialog. The ID should be unique for your landmark. If you enter an ID that is already the ID of some other landmark, you will be informed of that when you try to save your landmark.

Name

Every landmark has to have a name. The name is not unique, and can be in any form you like. It is entered at the second text field from the top on the left side of the dialog.

Description

You can write anything you want about your landmark in its description. The description is entered at the third field from the top on the left side of the dialog.

Climate

The climate can be Ice cap, Continental, Moderate continental, Subtropic, Tropic and Desert. You can choose climate from the upper combo-box at the left side of the dialog.

Tourist status

The Tourist status can be Exploated, Available and Not available. You can choose climate from the lower combo-box at the left side of the dialog.

Annual revenue

The Annual revenue represents amount of money the wonder earns, represented in U.S. Dollars. The revenue is entered at the text field below the combo-boxes on the left side of the dialog. You can only enter digits in this field. If you enter a non-digit, the border around the field will turn red as a mark of invalid entry. You will not be able to save your landmark if the entered revenue is not valid.

Informations

There are 3 check boxes on the dialog, with appropriate statements to the left. If you find that the statement is true for your landmark, you can check the check box to the right of the statement to mark this statement true, and uncheck it to mark the statement false. The default state of the check boxes is false.

Date of discovery

You can also enter the date your wonder was discovered in the date field at the top of the right side of the dialog.

You can either enter the date manually, in which case, you must enter it in the appropriate format, which is "dd/mmm/yyyy", where dd is the day of the month, mmm is the name of the month, or its three-letter abbreviation, and year is the year of the discovery.

If you want to choose the date from the calendar, you can click the calendar icon on the right side of the field and a calendar will appear below the field. At first, the current month will appear in the calendar, but you can switch between the months of the current year by clicking on the arrows at the sides of the calendar to navigate to month before and month after, or by clicking the name of the current month and choosing the desired month from the list of all months in current year. To switch between years, you can click the arrows at the sides of the mentioned list of the months, or by clicking at the year number at the top and choosing the year from the list of the years in the current decade. You can also navigate through the decades by clicking the arrows on the sides of the mentioned list of years in the decade.

Landmark Type

Every landmark must have a landmark type. Landmark types are created by you and you can choose a landmark type or create new landmark type for your landmark by clicking the 'Choose type' button on the right side of the form, or by pressing the Control+Y keys.

When you select a landmark type, its name will appear in the text field above the 'Choose type' button. You cannot enter anything in this field, but you can always change the type by clicking 'Choose type' button again. If you don't choose a landmark type, you will not be able to save your landmark.

Landmark picture

Landmarks placed on the map are presented in squares which are filled with their picture. You can choose a picture for your landmark by clicking the 'Browse' button and choosing a existing image file from the files on your device. Selected picture will appear at the left of the 'Browse' button.

If you have chosen a landmark type, but not the landmark picture, the picture of chosen landmark type will be placed as the landmark picture, so selecting the picture is not required.

Tags

Every landmark can have its tags. Tags are not mandatory - you don't have to tag your landmark if you don't want to. But if you want to add a tag to your landmark, you can do it in two ways. First, you can choose from existing tags or add a new tag by clicking the 'Manage tags' button at the bottom of the right side of the dialog, or by pressing Control+T keys.

If you already know the ID's of your tags, you can enter them manually in the landmark window in the text field on the bottom of the right side of the dialog. Existing tags are added to your landmark by typing the hash ('#') and tag's ID right after it. You can even type multiple tags, just be sure to separate them with a single space. If you have entered an invalid tag ID, you will be informed of that right after you leave the field where you entered them.
